Polite Meals

In appreciation for the food you've eaten, the Japanese tend to say "gochisousamadeshita," 「ごちそうさまでした」
It is a polite form of thanking someone for the meal you've received. The opposite, of course, is "ittadakimasu," 「いっただきます」
The intention is to be thankful of the meal you are about to enjoy.

Chopsticks　｜　おはし　｜　お箸

  • Avoid Pointing your chopsticks at someone while talking.
  • Do not wave your chopsticks around over food on the table.
  • Do not stab your food with your chopsticks

Drinking

While drinking, it is customary to serve one another rather than to pour your own drink.
Do not start drinking until everybody at the table has a drink and the glasses are raised for a drinking salute, which usually is "kampai," 「カンパイ」.
